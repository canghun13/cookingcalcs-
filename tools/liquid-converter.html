Common Liquid Conversions in Cooking

The most frequently needed conversions in everyday cooking are: 1 cup = 240ml, 1 tablespoon = 15ml, 1 teaspoon = 5ml, and 1 fluid ounce = 30ml. These are the four values worth memorizing — they come up constantly in both US and metric recipes.

For scaling recipes, the most useful thing to know is the relationship between tablespoons and cups: 16 tablespoons = 1 cup. So ¼ cup is 4 tablespoons, ⅓ cup is 5 tablespoons + 1 teaspoon, and ½ cup is 8 tablespoons.

Fluid Ounces vs. Weight Ounces

These are completely different units that happen to share the word "ounce." Fluid ounces measure volume — how much space a liquid takes up. Weight ounces measure mass. For water, they happen to be nearly equal: 1 fluid ounce of water weighs about 1.04 ounces. But for other liquids, they diverge significantly. 1 fluid ounce of honey weighs about 1.5 ounces because honey is much denser than water. Always check whether a recipe means fluid ounces (volume) or weight ounces when measuring liquids by ounces.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many ml in a cup?

1 US cup = 236.6ml, typically rounded to 240ml in cooking contexts. The metric cup (used in Australia and Canada) is exactly 250ml. For most recipes, the difference between 237ml and 240ml is negligible — use whichever is easier to measure with your equipment.

How many tablespoons in ⅓ cup?

⅓ cup equals 5 tablespoons + 1 teaspoon (79ml). This is one of the more awkward fractions because it doesn't divide evenly into tablespoons. To measure ⅓ cup without a ⅓ cup measure: use 5 tablespoons and then add 1 teaspoon more.

Is 8 fl oz the same as 1 cup?

Yes — in US measurements, 8 fluid ounces equals 1 cup (236.6ml). This is why an 8 oz glass of water is also described as 1 cup. The conversion 1 cup = 8 fl oz is one of the most reliable in US cooking measurement.

How do I convert a recipe from cups to ml?

Multiply the cup amount by 240 to get approximate milliliters, or by 236.6 for the precise conversion. For example: 1.5 cups × 240 = 360ml. For tablespoons: multiply by 15. For teaspoons: multiply by 5. These three conversions handle almost all liquid measurements in US recipes.
